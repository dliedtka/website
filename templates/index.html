Welcome to my page! My name is David Liedtka. I am a Cyber Warfare Engineer officer in the United States Navy who is interested in artificial intelligience, mostly machine and deep learning, and cyber security, specifically offensive capability development. I grew up in New Jersey and currently live in Washington, D.C.

I am a 2018 graduate of the United States Naval Academy, completing a dual-major Bachelor of Science in Computer Science and Information Technology. As a Trident Scholar at the Naval Academy, I conducted machine learning research with my adviser, Professor Luke K. McDowell, on using links between regions to predict elections as they unfold in real time. I also competed on the track and field team.

I am also a 2020 graduate of Stanford University, earning a Master of Science in Computer Science with a primary focus in artificial intelligience and a secondary focus in computer and network security. At Stanford, I was a member of the inaugural cohort of Knight-Hennessy Scholars.

I put this page together to highlight some of my past work and current personal projects. I'm always interested in hearing from people with (or without!) similar interests. I recently started converting this site to a dynamic rather than static website, a project that is still very much in progress, so please bear with me as I continue to add features incrementally.
